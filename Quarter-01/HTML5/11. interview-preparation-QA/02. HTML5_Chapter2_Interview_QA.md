# HTML5 Chapter 2 — 35 Industrial Interview Questions & Answers

This document contains **35 HTML5 industrial-level interview questions** with complete answers, code samples, production notes, follow‑up probes, difficulty levels, and evaluation rubric.

---

## ✅ 35 Industrial HTML5 Interview Questions & Answers

### 1. What is the purpose of `<!DOCTYPE html>`?
**Answer:** It ensures the browser renders the page in **HTML5 standards mode**, preventing quirks mode inconsistencies.  
**Code:** `<!DOCTYPE html>`  
**Follow-up:** What happens without DOCTYPE?  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Check DevTools → Rendering → Document Mode.

---

### 2. Why must the `<html>` tag include `lang="en"`?
**Answer:** It informs AT (screen readers), improves SEO, and helps UA determine language.  
**Code:** `<html lang="en">`  
**Follow-up:** Impact on indexing?  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Lighthouse flags missing `lang`.

---

### 3. What is inside the `<head>` tag and why is it critical?
**Answer:** Metadata, page title, SEO tags, scripts, styles. Improves performance and accessibility.  
**Code:** `<meta charset="UTF-8">`  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Place critical CSS first for fast FCP.

---

### 4. What does `<meta charset="UTF-8">` do?
Ensures proper character encoding, preventing broken text.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Always first meta tag.

---

### 5. Why is `<title>` important?
Defines page title, improves SEO, affects browser tabs and bookmarking.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Keep titles < 60 chars.

---

### 6. When should you use `<link>`?
For external CSS, preconnect, preload, favicons.  
**Code:** `<link rel="stylesheet" href="style.css">`  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tip:** Use `rel="preload"` for font optimization.

---

### 7. Why load JavaScript at the bottom or with `defer`?
Improves page performance by preventing render-blocking.  
**Code:** `<script src="app.js" defer></script>`  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tip:** Avoid `async` for scripts that depend on DOM order.

---

### 8. Purpose of `<body>`?
Holds all visible UI and interactive elements.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Keep body clean and semantic.

---

### 9. When to use `<header>`?
Top section of page/section for branding/nav.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Avoid placing headings inside non-semantic wrappers.

---

### 10. When to use `<footer>`?
Bottom info, copyright, related links.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Every major section may have its own footer.

---

### 11. Difference between `<nav>` and generic links?
`<nav>` wraps major navigation groups for accessibility.  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Code:** `<nav><a href="#">Home</a></nav>`  
**Tip:** Screen readers detect nav landmarks.

---

### 12. Why use `<section>`?
Groups related thematic content.  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tip:** Must contain a heading for semantic clarity.

---

### 13. Difference between `<article>` and `<section>`?
`<article>` is standalone; `<section>` groups related topics.  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tip:** Blogs → use article per post.

---

### 14. When should `<div>` be used?
Only when no semantic element fits.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Replace div soup with proper tags.

---

### 15. Difference between `<span>` and `<div>`?
`<span>` is inline; `<div>` is block-level.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Use span for inline styling only.

---

### 16. Correct use of headings `<h1>–<h6>`?
Hierarchical, semantic structure. One logical `h1`.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Avoid jumping levels (h1 → h4).

---

### 17. Purpose of `<p>`?
Paragraph-level text container.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Don’t nest block elements inside `<p>`.

---

### 18. How do `<b>` and `<strong>` differ?
`<strong>` conveys importance; `<b>` is visual styling.  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tip:** Prefer `<strong>` for accessibility.

---

### 19. How do `<i>` and `<em>` differ?
`<em>` adds emphasis; `<i>` is stylistic.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Prefer `<em>` for meaning.

---

### 20. Use of `<sub>` and `<sup>`?
For chemical formulas, mathematical expressions.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Helps screen reader interpretation.

---

### 21. When to use `<small>`?
For legal text, disclaimers, minor notes.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner

---

### 22. When to use `<mark>`?
Highlighting relevant search context or matched text.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Avoid overuse; affects contrast.

---

### 23. When is `<br>` appropriate?
Poetry, addresses, not layout.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Never use `<br>` to create spacing.

---

### 24. Role of `<hr>`?
Semantic thematic break, not visual line.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner

---

### 25. When to use `<blockquote>`?
Long quotations with optional citation.  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

---

### 26. Use of `<q>`?
Inline short quotations; adds quotation marks automatically.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner

---

### 27. Purpose of `<abbr>`?
Defines abbreviations with tooltips.  
**Code:** `<abbr title="World Health Organization">WHO</abbr>`  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tip:** Helps SEO and accessibility.

---

### 28. Use of `<cite>`?
Names of creative works.  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

---

### 29. Use of `<dfn>`?
Mark term being defined.  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

---

### 30. Purpose of `<address>`?
Author/contact information.  
**Difficulty:** Beginner

---

### 31. Difference between `<del>` and `<s>`?
`<del>` = removed content; `<s>` = outdated but still relevant.  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

---

### 32. Why avoid excessive `<b>`, `<i>`, `<u>`?
They provide no semantic meaning.  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tip:** Prefer semantic formatting.

---

### 33. Why semantic HTML improves SEO?
Search engines understand structure and ranking signals.  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tip:** Use heading hierarchy.

---

### 34. How screen readers interpret semantic tags?
Landmarks improve navigation for visually impaired users.  
**Difficulty:** Advanced

---

### 35. Why avoid using `<div>` for headings or nav?
Breaks semantics, harms accessibility tools.  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Tip:** Validate with WAVE extension.

# Evaluation Rubric
Each question scored **0–2 points**:
- **0:** Incorrect / no awareness  
- **1:** Partial correctness / missing production reasoning  
- **2:** Complete + practical + semantically accurate
